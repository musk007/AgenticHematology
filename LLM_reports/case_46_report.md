Absent cytoplasmic vacuoles in the blast cohort does not negate monocytic differentiation but makes the morphology less classically monoblastic.

**Diagnostic flags:** blasts present; left shifted myeloid.

**Impression:** Acute Myeloid Leukemia with monocytic differentiation (suspected).

Monocytic-lineage predominance (promonocytes 83.8%) with circulating blasts (8.7%) is suspicious for a monocytic AML pattern under sparse annotation conditions.

**Differential considerations:**
- Acute monoblastic/monocytic leukemia (AML-M5).
- Acute myelomonocytic leukemia (AML-M4).
- Reactive monocytosis with immature forms (less likely).

**Recommended workup:**
- Flow cytometric immunophenotyping (CD64, CD14, CD11b, lysozyme, CD33).
- Bone marrow aspirate and trephine biopsy.
- Cytogenetics and targeted molecular AML panel.
